What is a Condo?

A condominium, or condo, is a type of real estate where individual units are privately owned, but shared spaces—like hallways, pools, and recreation areas—are collectively owned by all the condo owners within the complex. Condos offer a unique combination of homeownership and community living, providing residents with the privacy of owning their unit while enjoying amenities without the hassle of maintaining them.

The Appeal and Flexibility of Condos

First-Time Homebuyers

Condos can be a more affordable entry into homeownership, especially in urban areas where single-family homes may be financially out of reach.

Retirees

Many retirees choose condos for their convenience. HOA fees cover maintenance and upkeep, allowing retirees to enjoy a low-maintenance lifestyle.

Young Professionals

For young professionals, condos in urban areas offer the convenience of being close to work, entertainment, and dining options.

Real Estate Investors

Condos present a solid investment opportunity with reliable rental income potential.

Challenges of Selling a Condo

Selling a condo comes with some unique challenges due to the involvement of homeowners’ associations (HOAs). HOAs may have specific rules and fees that could impact the sale of your property...

Factors That Influence Condo Value

  • Location: Like all real estate, location is a crucial factor in determining a condo’s value.
  • Size and Layout: The square footage of your condo and its layout influence its marketability.
  • Condition of the Property: Although GIGI Buys Homes buys condos as-is, the property’s condition still plays a role in determining our offer.
  • HOA Fees and Rules: High HOA fees can deter buyers by adding to the cost of ownership.
  • Market Trends: Real estate market conditions impact condo prices.
